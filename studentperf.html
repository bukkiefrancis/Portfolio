Data Analytics Project

Factors Influencing Student Academic Performance

This project explores how behavioural, lifestyle and environmental factors influence student academic performance. Using Excel for cleaning, SQL for analysis and Power BI for visualisation, the project identifies patterns in study habits, attendance, sleep and parental involvement to understand which factors are most strongly linked to exam scores.

Project Overview

Educational outcomes are often shaped by more than just study time. This analysis was carried out to examine the relationship between academic performance and wider factors such as attendance, sleep habits, access to resources and parental support.

The aim was to move beyond simple averages and uncover meaningful patterns that could support better decision-making and student support strategies.

Objectives

  • Analyse how study time relates to exam performance
  • Examine the impact of attendance on academic outcomes
  • Assess whether lifestyle factors such as sleep influence scores
  • Explore how parental involvement and access to resources affect performance
  • Present clear insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard

Tools Used

Excel

Used for data cleaning, validation and preparation before analysis.

SQL

Used to analyse trends, group categories and calculate summary metrics.

Power BI

Used to create an interactive dashboard for visualising key findings.

Data Preparation

The dataset was reviewed in Excel to check for missing values, data consistency and formatting. Fields were assessed to ensure they were suitable for analysis and visualisation.

Additional grouping was used where helpful to support interpretation, such as comparing performance across categories of attendance, sleep hours and study time.

SQL Analysis

SQL queries were used to explore relationships between academic performance and the key factors in the dataset.

Average Exam Score by Study Hours

SELECT
    Study_Hours_Per_Day,
    ROUND(AVG(Exam_Score), 2) AS avg_exam_score
FROM student_performance
GROUP BY Study_Hours_Per_Day
ORDER BY Study_Hours_Per_Day;

Average Exam Score by Attendance

SELECT
    Attendance,
    ROUND(AVG(Exam_Score), 2) AS avg_exam_score
FROM student_performance
GROUP BY Attendance
ORDER BY Attendance;

Average Exam Score by Sleep Hours

SELECT
    Sleep_Hours_Per_Day,
    ROUND(AVG(Exam_Score), 2) AS avg_exam_score
FROM student_performance
GROUP BY Sleep_Hours_Per_Day
ORDER BY Sleep_Hours_Per_Day;

Exam Score by Parental Involvement

SELECT
    Parental_Involvement,
    ROUND(AVG(Exam_Score), 2) AS avg_exam_score
FROM student_performance
GROUP BY Parental_Involvement
ORDER BY avg_exam_score DESC;

Dashboard Overview

A Power BI dashboard was developed to make the findings easier to explore visually. It includes KPI cards, trend charts and category comparisons to help identify the strongest drivers of student performance.

  • KPI cards showing average exam score and other headline metrics
  • Charts comparing study hours, attendance and sleep patterns
  • Breakdowns by parental involvement and other student factors
  • Interactive filters to explore patterns across the dataset

Key Insights

Study Time Matters

Students who spent more time studying generally achieved stronger exam results, showing a positive relationship between study habits and academic performance.

Attendance Has Strong Influence

Higher attendance was associated with better scores, suggesting that consistent engagement plays a major role in student success.

Lifestyle Balance Is Important

Sleep patterns also appeared to affect performance, indicating that academic outcomes are influenced by lifestyle as well as study effort.

Support Systems Contribute

Factors such as parental involvement may also influence outcomes, highlighting the importance of a supportive learning environment.

Conclusion

This project shows that student performance is shaped by a combination of academic, behavioural and environmental factors. While increased study time supports stronger results, attendance and lifestyle habits also appear to play a key role.

By combining structured analysis with interactive reporting, this project demonstrates how data can be used to uncover patterns, support evidence-based decisions and communicate findings clearly to different audiences.
